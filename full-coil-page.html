Why Coil Securement Matters

Boxcars in motion generate longitudinal, lateral, and vertical forces. Without proper securement, coils can shift, risking safety, cargo damage, and delays. Traditional bulkhead systems add weight and reduce payload. Modern approaches that combine web straps or non-metallic strapping with anti-slip mats provide a lighter, safer, and more efficient alternative to heavy blocking and bracing.

Products for Closed-Car Coil Programs

4″ Web Strap Assemblies

Holistic support for current coil loading methods using 4″ web assemblies. Assemblies are anchored to floor hardware and tensioned with ratchets; corner/edge protection is applied at coil contact points. We supply 4″ web assemblies today and support programs that plan to transition to alternative systems.

  • Base and diagonal configurations (per published methods)
  • Compatible with coil edge protection
  • Fast to install and release for efficient turnarounds

Grade 7 Non-Metallic Strapping

Polyester cord strapping used as supplemental or encircling restraint where specified. Applied with approved buckles and tensioners; lightweight, flexible, and operator-friendly. Placement and configuration follow published practices; detailed engineering is shared within trials.

  • Encircling / supplemental applications
  • Edge protection at coil contact points
  • Training and site implementation available

Grade 8 Non-Metallic Strapping (Field Trials)

Next-generation alternative now entering closed-car coil trials in collaboration with Class I railroads and the AAR. Designed as a high-strength option to simplify coil securement versus legacy harnesses—while maintaining compliance and safety objectives.

  • Engineered as an alternative to 4″ web assemblies
  • Lightweight, easy handling in the field
  • Participation via immediate field trials

Anti-Slip Rubber Mats

AAR-approved rubber friction mats positioned beneath coil skids or pallets to increase interface grip and help prevent sliding. Formats support coverage beyond pallet edges and doorway areas as appropriate. Durability enables use across multiple load cycles.

  • AAR-approved manufacturers
  • Integrates with pallet/skid systems
  • Supports modern strapping approaches

Edge & Corner Protection

Protects strap/webbing at coil contact points and maintains system performance. Options include CEP2, CEP16, edgeboards, and flexboards.

  • Reduces abrasion and point loading
  • Supports compliance and longevity
  • Fits a variety of coil diameters and skids

Coil Loading Best Practices

  1. Inspect the boxcar for sound floors, anchors, and watertight seals.
  2. Place anti-slip rubber mats beneath coil skids/pallets to increase grip.
  3. Apply web straps or non-metallic straps per the chosen method and specification.
  4. Use edge protection wherever straps contact coil edges.
  5. Balance loads, fill appropriate voids, and verify restraint before release.
  6. Use doorway retention options where receiver opening safety is a concern.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. 1 Do you still supply 4″ web assemblies?

    Yes. We supply certified 4″ web assemblies and support mixed-fleet programs. In parallel, we’re collaborating with Class I railroads and the AAR on alternative pathways for closed-car coil securement.

  2. 2 Are anti-slip mats required?

    AAR-approved anti-slip rubber mats are widely used in closed-car coil programs to improve interface grip and reduce shifting risk. Specific usage depends on the method and car conditions.

  3. 3 What’s the difference between Grade 7/8 and web assemblies?

    Grade 7/8 are non-metallic cord strapping solutions used in defined applications and field trials. Web assemblies are 4″ wide systems with ratchets and anchor hardware used in established methods. We support both, depending on your program.

  4. 4 Will you share calculations and test data?

    Detailed engineering, calculations, and test data are provided within approved field trials and AAR review—not on this public page.
